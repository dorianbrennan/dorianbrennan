Port Macquarie Helipad

Sydney  ·  2022 – 2023

Healthcare  ·  $15M

NBRS was engaged to relocate the existing ground-level helipad at Port Macquarie Base Hospital to a state-of-the-art rooftop helipad positioned above the current Emergency Department. The former helipad was located to the east of the hospital site, surrounded by at-grade parking and separated from the Emergency Department, operating theatres and inpatient (IPU) wards. This separation compromised the efficiency and safety of patient transfers. The relocation delivered a new helipad constructed on Level 4 above the Emergency Department on Level 2. To ensure seamless patient transfer from the helipad to the emergency treatment areas, a dedicated passenger/bed lift was designed, along with an enclosed pedestrian link and patient transfer corridor. These improvements enhance control, safety and response times, enabling clinical staff to commence treatment more quickly.

Role I joined the project shortly after the completion of the initial Design Development (DD) phase and was appointed as Project Architect to lead the project through tender documentation, construction and site attendance. My role involved managing a team of four to develop and coordinate the documentation from DD through to tender and into construction. I also attended user group meetings and coordinated closely with the consultant team. Constructing the project above an operational Emergency Department and integrating it with the existing hospital presented significant challenges. These complexities were successfully addressed through close collaboration with the broader consultant team and the builder.

The Grove

Randwick NSW  ·  2013-2016

Aged Care  ·  $60M

Add image
Add image

The project comprises 79 retirement living apartments, ranging from one to three bedrooms, and a 113-room residential care facility. Community facilities include a chapel, day care centre, multi-purpose hall, swimming pool, exercise rooms, allied health suites, and a café. The site forms part of the curtilage of the historic Brigidine Convent, with more than 50% of the land covered by magnificent fig trees. It is located in the centre of The Spot village. Sustainable design considerations balance resident comfort with enhanced as-built and operational energy efficiency. These initiatives include a predominantly north/northeast orientation for the majority of apartments; landscaped roof terraces; material selection based on environmental performance; the provision of natural light and ventilation to almost all habitable and circulation areas; and water storage and reuse for landscape irrigation.

Role Roles included DA drawings, tender drawings, consultant coordination, construction documentation, and site attendance.
